Exam advice

What actually helps when preparing

📌 Start from the beginning

Studying chapters in order is the foundation of understanding. The material follows the sequence set by the official CISI syllabus: each topic builds on the previous one.

🔤 Learn the exact wording

CISI tests knowledge of precise legal and regulatory definitions. "Roughly the same thing" does not work. Learn exact wording.

⚠️ Watch for similar answer options

The exam deliberately includes 2–3 plausible-sounding options. The trainer teaches you to spot the critical difference.

🔁 Use the wrong-answer mode

Don't repeat what you already know. Work through your error bank — the most effective use of your time in the days before the exam.

📄 Complete all official sample papers

Official CISI sample papers (v28, v29, v31, v32) are built into the trainer. Sitting them in real-time mode (75 questions / 90 min) trains your pace.

📅 Allow enough time

CISI estimates around 80 hours — for someone already working in finance with confident English. At B1–B2 level, realistic preparation time is 120–150 hours. Two to four months at a comfortable pace is an honest target.

Frequently asked questions

FAQ

Who is this course for?
For anyone preparing to sit the CISI UK Financial Regulation exam (Level 3). It forms part of the IOC (Investment Operations Certificate), the Capital Markets Programme, and several other CISI qualifications. The course suits both those already working in financial services and those entering the industry.
How up to date is the material?
The course is based on the official CISI textbook Edition 33 (January 2026), valid for exams from 1 April 2026 to 31 March 2027. This is the latest available edition. The material includes Consumer Duty 2024, post-Brexit UK MAR, and all recent regulatory changes.
How long does preparation take?
CISI estimates the textbook requires approximately 80 hours — for someone already working in finance who reads English confidently. If your English is at B1–B2 level and the subject matter is unfamiliar, realistic preparation time easily reaches 120–150 hours or more: part of that time goes on the language itself, not just the content. Two to four months of sessions three times a week is an honest and comfortable target.
What is the format of the real CISI exam?
The exam is a Computer Based Test (CBT): 75 multiple-choice questions, 90 minutes. The pass mark is approximately 70% (~52–53 correct answers). Questions are drawn from 4 elements: Chapter 1 (~12 questions), Chapter 2 (~35 questions), Chapter 3 (~19 questions), Chapter 4 (~9 questions). Calculators are not permitted — only the on-screen calculator.
Are there Russian translations in the study guides?
Yes. The study guides contain English text with an interpreted Russian translation. This lets you study the content and absorb professional English terminology in context at the same time.
How much does this course improve your chances of passing?
An honest answer: the course provides the highest-quality preparation possible — structured material, 1,100+ practice questions, official sample papers and live sessions with a tutor. But results are always a partnership: the tutor is responsible for the quality of preparation; the student is responsible for pace, consistency and engagement. Rushing the timeline rarely helps.
Are official CISI sample papers included?
Yes. The trainer includes official CISI sample papers — Version 28, Version 29, Version 31 and Version 32 (75 questions each). A dedicated "Sample Papers" mode lets you select the version and number of questions per round.
